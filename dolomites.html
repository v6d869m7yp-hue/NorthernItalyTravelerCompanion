Two-night plan

Use the weather, not the clock.

Mountain visibility matters more than rigid sequencing. Put the clearest morning toward Seceda and use Alpe di Siusi or a scenic drive when conditions are softer.

Arrival day · Sept 2

Settle into Ortisei

  • DriveLeave Venice and watch the landscape change into the Alps.
  • ArrivalCheck in, orient yourself and confirm lift operating times.
  • EveningWalk Ortisei, have an early dinner and check the next morning's forecast.
Full mountain day · Sept 3

Seceda first, Alpe di Siusi second

  • MorningRide the lift to Seceda while visibility is best.
  • MiddayModerate ridge or meadow walk; lunch at a mountain hut.
  • AfternoonAlpe di Siusi, Passo Gardena or a shorter scenic option depending on energy.

Three good choices.

Keep the hikes flexible. Elevation, weather and lift timing can make a short walk feel much more demanding than the mileage suggests.

Scenic roads

Let the road be the experience.

Passo Gardena and the broader Dolomite road network can provide an outstanding afternoon without committing to another major hike.

Passo Gardena

A high-value scenic drive close to Ortisei, with frequent opportunities to stop without turning the day into a major expedition.

The Great Dolomite Road

Use selected portions rather than trying to complete every pass. The best drive is the one that still leaves time to stop and look.

Mountain rule

If the peaks are clear, go up. If clouds settle in, enjoy Ortisei, mountain huts, lower meadows or a scenic drive and try the lift again later.

Before leaving Ortisei

Prepare for Cinque Terre.

This is the longest and most demanding transfer in the itinerary. Leave with a full tank, a realistic lunch stop and enough margin for mountain and motorway traffic.
